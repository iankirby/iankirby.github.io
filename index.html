    General

    I'm a fifth year PhD student in linguistics at Harvard University. My main research interests are semantics, morphology, and the syntax-semantics interface. One area of particular interest to me is the morphosyntactic expression of polarity-sensitive (and free-choice) elements, particularly when languages uses quantifier particles to build these elements. I have a special interest in Turkic languages, particularly the understudied Siberian branch. I serve as the Department Writing Fellow for Linguistics and am also an associate at the Davis Center for Russian and Eurasian Studies.
